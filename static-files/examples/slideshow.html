Thunderbird Contacts Add-on
mozillalabs.com/messaging/thunderbird-contacts

Contacts is an experiment with a new look on how the Thunderbird address book could evolve. Born from the Mozilla Labs Contacts in Firefox add-on, Thunderbird Contacts was adapted to work in Thunderbird and includes some new features.

Thunderbird Contacts lets you pull in your “web contacts” from sites like LinkedIn, Facebook, and Twitter. It also lets you connect to your mail contacts inside of Yahoo or GMail. As each contact is extracted from a site, the Contacts add-on tries to merge the contacts from different sites together into a single contact on your system.

Learn more: http://mozillalabs.com/messaging/thunderbird-contacts/

Firefox 4

Where are we now?

380K ADUs, we want to have between 2-4 M ADUs before launch

This is what a current Beta user looks like:

  • More than 85% of beta users have used Firefox for more than 3 years
  • More than 70% of users are between 18-35
  • Nearly 96% of Beta users are male
  • Nearly 50% of Beta users use the internet between 2-6 hours a day
  • 70% of users describe their skill level as an 8-10 on a 10 point scale.
  • 70% of users use more than one browser in daily life
  • Almost 70% of those that use more than one browser identify Firefox as their primary browser
  • Most people use the internet for entertainment and communication
  • Search, News, Social Networking, Webmail are the main types of sites beta users go to
  • Most people find out about latest news from online news sources and online blogs
  • Most users use en-US, de, es*, fr, ru or pt-BR locales
